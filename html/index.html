Coffee is one of the most popular beverages in the world. It is made from roasted coffee beans, which are the seeds of berries from the Coffea plant. The two most common types of coffee beans are Arabica and Robusta.

The process of making coffee involves several steps, including grinding the beans, brewing, and serving. The ICO oversees the global coffee trade and ensures quality standards are met.

Warning: Consuming too much coffee can lead to health issues such as insomnia and increased heart rate. It is recommended to limit your intake to a moderate amount.

Many people enjoy coffee for its rich flavor and stimulating effects. As the famous quote goes, Good communication is just as stimulating as black coffee, and just as hard to sleep after.

In recent years, there has been a growing interest in specialty coffee, which focuses on high-quality beans and precise brewing methods. This trend has led to the rise of third-wave coffee shops, which emphasize the mass production artisanal approach to coffee making.

For more detailed information, you can refer to the book The World Atlas of Coffee by James Hoffmann.

For more information about coffee, visit Coffee.org.

The optimal brewing temperature T can be estimated using the following formula: T = E − S V where E is the extraction efficiency, S is the solubility of coffee, and V is the volume of water.

The ideal coffee-to-water ratio R can be expressed as: R = M V where M is the mass of coffee grounds, and V is the volume of water.
